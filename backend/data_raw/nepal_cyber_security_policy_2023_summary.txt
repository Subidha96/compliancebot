Source: National Cyber Security Policy, 2023 (Nepal Cabinet, August 2023)
Official: https://mocit.gov.np/content/7119/7119-national-cyber-security-policy/
================================================================================

This document quotes the actual policy text from the Ministry of Communication
and Information Technology (MoCIT)'s official English translation, not a
synthetic paraphrase.

1. BACKGROUND
   With the rapid advancement of information technology, significant changes
   are occurring in social interaction, public service delivery, and the flow
   of information. With the continuous development of information
   technology, its growing use, and increased mobility, unauthorized access
   to information and related risks have also increased, making cyber
   security a national priority.

2. LONG-TERM VISION
   Building a secure as well as Resilient Cyber Space.

3. MISSION
   To ensure information and data security and protection of information
   technology systems through a combination of people, process and
   technology by developing legal and institutional structure, enhancing
   public awareness and developing capacity.

4. TARGET
   To increase Nepal's Global Cyber Security Index (GCI) score from the
   then-current 44.99 to 60% within the next 5 years, 70% within 10 years,
   and 80% within 15 years.

5. OBJECTIVES
   9.1 Establish legal and institutional frameworks to build a secure cyber
       space.
   9.2 Protect critical national infrastructure while reducing the risk of
       cyber-attacks.
   9.3 Enhance cyber security research, human resource development and
       enhancing capacity of existing workforce, to ensure a robust and
       strong cyber space.
   9.4 Improve the reliability and security of digital technology-based
       services.
   9.5 Exchange bilateral, regional and international coordination, knowledge
       sharing, and assistance to reduce cyber security related risks.

6. STRATEGIES
   10.1 Formulation of necessary laws and standards for a secure and
        resilient cyber space.
   10.2 Establish and strengthen institutional structures to protect
        information and information and communication technology systems.
   10.3 Identification and protection of critical national infrastructure
        while managing robust and strong technology, infrastructure and
        systems to reform cyber security.
   10.4 Develop, research and utilize the competent human resources related
        to cyber security.
   10.5 Operation of digital literacy programs to enhance awareness related
        to cyber security.
   10.6 Coordination and collaboration among public agencies, private
        sectors, and civil society to construct secure cyber space.
   10.7 Cooperation with foreign countries and international organizations
        to improve cyber security.
   10.8 Continuous monitoring for cyber security to construct safe online
        space.
   10.9 Holding software developers or suppliers, hardware manufacturers or
        suppliers, or service providers accountable as required.

7. CRITICAL INFORMATION INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION
   The policy establishes the National Cyber Security Center under MoCIT and
   introduces strategic objectives for protecting critical information
   infrastructure (CII) — including banking, energy, telecommunications, and
   government systems — from cyber-attacks, and for developing systems to
   detect, prevent, and respond to cyber threats in real time.

8. ACTION PLAN
   The policy lays out an action plan tied to each strategy above, including:
   amending and refining existing laws to align with cyber security,
   enacting laws to control cyber-crime, formulating a National Cyber
   Security Framework, conducting critical infrastructure risk assessments,
   and formulating business continuity and disaster recovery procedures.
