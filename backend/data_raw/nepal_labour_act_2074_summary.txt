VERBATIM EXCERPT — The Labour Act, 2074 (2017), Key Provisions
Source: Nepal Law Commission
Official: https://lawcommission.gov.np
================================================================================

The Labour Act 2074 (2017) is the principal legislation governing employment
relationships, workplace rights, and employer obligations in Nepal. It replaced
the Labour Act 2048 (1992) and applies to all enterprises, companies, factories,
and establishments employing one or more workers, including foreign-invested
entities operating in Nepal.

1. APPLICABILITY AND SCOPE

   Section 2: Application
   - The Act applies to all enterprises, companies, cooperatives, and
     organizations operating under Nepalese company laws.
   - It also applies to foreign-registered entities engaged in business,
     sales, or promotional activities in Nepal.
   - Non-applicability: Civil Service, Nepal Army, Nepal Police, Armed
     Police Force, and working journalists (unless specified in contract).

2. TYPES OF EMPLOYMENT

   Section 10: Employment Categories
   - Regular Employment: Hired for ongoing work and services.
   - Work-Based Employment: Hired for a specific task or service.
   - Time-Bound Employment: Employment for a certain time period.
   - Casual Employment: Employment for 7 days or fewer in a month.
   - Part-Time Employment: Employment for 35 hours or less per week
     with pay based on hours worked.

3. WORKER RIGHTS AND PROTECTIONS

   Section 4: Fundamental Rights
   - Right to work and fair employment.
   - Right to equal pay for equal work regardless of gender.
   - Right to a safe and healthy working environment.
   - Right to form and join trade unions.
   - Right to collective bargaining.
   - Right to social security.

   Section 5: Prohibitions
   - Forced labour under coercion or threats is strictly prohibited.
   - Employment of children (under 18 years) is prohibited.
   - Discrimination based on gender, caste, religion, or other grounds
     is prohibited in hiring, promotion, or termination.
   - Unequal pay for equal work based on gender is prohibited.

4. WORKING HOURS AND CONDITIONS

   Section 30: Working Hours
   - Standard working hours: 8 hours per day, 48 hours per week.
   - A 30-minute break must be provided after every 5 hours of work.
   - If work cannot be interrupted, employees must work in shifts with
     breaks in between.

   Section 31: Overtime
   - Overtime hours: Maximum 24 hours per week, not exceeding 4 hours per day.
   - Overtime pay: 1.5 times the regular hourly rate.

   Section 32: Weekly Rest
   - Every employee is entitled to at least 1 day of rest per week.

5. WAGES AND REMUNERATION

   Section 35: Minimum Wage
   - The minimum wage rate is NPR 17,300 per month (as of August 2023).
   - Tea estate workers: NPR 13,893 per month.
   - The government reviews and updates the minimum wage periodically.

   Section 36: Payment Terms
   - Short-term work: Payment within 3 days of completion.
   - Regular employees: Payment at month-end.
   - Annual increment: Half-day salary for employees working at least 1 year.

6. LEAVE ENTITLEMENTS

   Section 40-45: Leave Provisions
   - Annual leave: 30 days per year after completing 1 year of service.
   - Sick leave: 15 days per year.
   - Maternity leave: 98 days (52 days before and 46 days after delivery).
   - Paternity leave: 5 days.
   - Bereavement leave: 5 days for immediate family members.
   - Women employees are entitled to an additional 18 days of maternity
     leave if they have more than 2 children.

7. SOCIAL SECURITY

   Section 60-70: Social Security Fund
   - All employers must contribute to the Social Security Fund on behalf
     of their employees.
   - Contributions: Employer contributes 11% of basic salary; employee
     contributes 11% of basic salary.
   - The fund provides:
     (a) Provident Fund — retirement savings.
     (b) Gratuity — one month's salary per year of service upon separation.
     (c) Health insurance — medical coverage for employees and dependents.
     (d) Accident and disability insurance — coverage for work-related
         injuries and disabilities.

8. WORKPLACE SAFETY AND HEALTH

   Section 72-78: Safety Provisions
   - Every employer must provide a safe and healthy working environment.
   - Where 20 or more employees are engaged, the employer must constitute
     a Safety and Health Committee.
   - Employers must provide personal protective equipment (PPE) at no cost
     to employees working in hazardous conditions.
   - Employers must report workplace injuries and fatalities to the Labour
     Office within 24 hours.

9. COLLECTIVE BARGAINING AND TRADE UNIONS

   Section 116: Collective Bargaining Committee
   - Where 10 or more employees are engaged, the employer must constitute
     a Collective Bargaining Committee.
   - The committee negotiates wages, working conditions, and other terms
     of employment on behalf of employees.

   Section 117: Right to Strike
   - Employees may strike if disputes remain unresolved after arbitration.
   - A 7-day prior notice to management and security agencies is mandatory.
   - Strikes are prohibited in essential services.

10. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

    Section 150-160: Labour Disputes
    - Individual claims: Settled through employer negotiation or via the
      Labour Office.
    - Collective claims: Submitted by a bargaining committee or trade union.
    - Mandatory arbitration: Required for essential services and SEZ entities.
    - Labour Court: Deals with serious violations including forced labour,
      workplace injuries, and disputes.
    - Cases must be resolved within 90 days.
    - Appeals can be made to the Supreme Court within 35 days.

11. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

    Section 170-180: Offences and Penalties
    - Employing minors (under 18): Fine of NPR 50,000 to NPR 100,000.
    - Forced labour: Imprisonment for up to 5 years or fine up to
      NPR 500,000, or both.
    - Gender discrimination: Fine of NPR 25,000 to NPR 100,000.
    - Failure to provide safe workplace: Fine of NPR 10,000 to NPR 50,000
      per violation.
    - Repeated violations: Higher penalties and potential license suspension.

RELEVANCE TO GRC:
   - Labour compliance is a critical component of organizational governance
     and regulatory compliance.
   - Companies must integrate labour law compliance into their GRC framework
     alongside cybersecurity, privacy, and financial regulations.
   - For women in Nepal's tech workforce (the primary users of ComplianceBot+),
     understanding maternity leave, equal pay, anti-discrimination, and
     workplace safety rights is essential.
