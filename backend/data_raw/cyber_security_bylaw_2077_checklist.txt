VERBATIM EXCERPT — official Byelaw text
Source: Cyber Security Byelaw, 2077 (2020), framed by Nepal Telecommunications
Authority (NTA) under Section 62 of the Telecommunication Act, 2053 (1997)
Official: https://nta.gov.np/uploads/contents/Cyber-Security-Bylaw-2077-2020.pdf
================================================================================

This document quotes the actual clause text/numbers from the Byelaw, not a
paraphrase. "Licensee" means the Telecommunication Service Provider (TSP) or
Internet Service Provider (ISP) holding a license issued by NTA, to whom this
Byelaw applies mandatorily.

1. ORGANIZATIONAL SECURITY (Chapter 2, clauses 3-24)
   Clause 4: Licensee shall have its board-approved ICT Security Policies for
     planning, organizing, and managing information security.
   Clause 5: Licensee shall review ICT Security Policies developed at least
     once a year.
   Clause 9: Licensee shall promote information security awareness throughout
     the organization.
   Clause 12: Licensee shall enforce password policy rules (length,
     complexity, rotation) for implementing password management.

2. NETWORK AND TECHNICAL CONTROLS (Chapters 3-6, clauses 25-52)
   Clause 25: Licensee shall use DDoS Detection and Mitigation systems.
   Clause 30: Licensee shall have provision to securely authenticate users
     (2-Factor Authentication/MFA).
   Clause 34: Licensee shall have Network Firewall and implement specified
     firewall measures.
   Clause 41: System clocks of all information processing systems shall be
     synchronized.
   Clause 44: Licensee shall comply with provisions related to log records.
   Clause 46: Licensee shall use OTP or other 2-factor authentication.
   Clause 48: Licensee shall use SSL/TLS in applicable services.
   Clause 50: Licensee shall apply encryption techniques for data in transit.

3. INFORMATION SYSTEM (IS) AUDIT — CHAPTER 7 (clauses 53-56)
   Clause 53: Licensee shall have an internal security audit team for
     regular network/system/critical infrastructure audit and submit the
     security audit report to the authority every six (6) months.
   Clause 54: Licensee shall perform penetration testing and vulnerability
     assessment every three (3) months and perform required rectifications.
   Clause 55: Licensee shall perform an Information System (IS) audit
     annually to verify compliance, conducted by NTA or a Government of
     Nepal-designated IS Auditor.
   Clause 56: Licensee shall rectify vulnerabilities and gaps identified by
     IS Auditors at the soonest.

4. CLOUD SECURITY — CHAPTER 8 (clause 57)
   Clause 57: Licensees providing cloud services shall define a cloud
   control matrix and zero-trust policy, require SLA/NDA with cloud service
   providers, use IAM for access control, and have cloud services audited
   annually by an NTA-designated Cloud Auditor.

5. CERT / INCIDENT RESPONSE — CHAPTER 9 (clauses 58-63)
   Clause 58: Licensee shall form an Incident Response team/CERT.
   Clause 59: Licensee shall have approved emergency incident response plans
     and communication plans.
   Clause 60: In case of any security incident, the licensee shall
     coordinate with NTA's task-force (chaired by NTA's Director, Monitoring
     Division) to minimize loss and identify the attack source.
   Clause 61-62: The task-force reports to a high-level NTA Steering
     Committee (chaired by the NTA Chairman) which issues directives and
     recommends policy decisions.
   Clause 63: NTA shall develop an Information Sharing Platform for
     incidents/threats, which licensees must use.

6. SECURITY OPERATIONS — CHAPTER 10 (clauses 64-68)
   Clause 64: Licensee shall have a dedicated in-house Security
     Unit/Operation Centre.
   Clause 65: All security alerts and events must be logged and monitored
     24x7.
   Clause 66: Licensee shall have systems like SIEM (Security Information
     and Event Management).

[VERIFY] Chapters 1, 6 (data protection), and 11 (training) and the full
Annex-1 audit checklist referenced in Clause 55 are not reproduced verbatim
here for length — consult the official NTA PDF for complete text.
