VERBATIM EXCERPT — official Act text
Source: The Privacy Act, 2075 (2018), Act Number 14 of 2075
Official: https://lawcommission.gov.np/content/12261/12261-the-privacy-act-2075/
================================================================================

This document quotes the actual Act text by chapter and section, not a
paraphrase. Note: the Act is structured around named categories of privacy
(body, residence, property, document, data, correspondence, character,
electronic means) rather than a GDPR-style "personal data" framework — do not
conflate the two when answering questions about Nepal-specific privacy law.

1. PREAMBLE
   An Act made to promote dignified living standards by ensuring the right to
   privacy of matters relating to body, residence, property, document, data,
   correspondence and character of every person, and to manage the
   protection and safe use of personal information held by public bodies.

2. CHAPTER 9 — ELECTRONIC MEANS AND PRIVACY (Sections 19-22)
   Section 19: Every person has the right to maintain privacy of personal
     information, documents, correspondence, data, or character held in
     electronic means. No one shall obtain, access, or disclose such
     material without authorization. No one shall record or listen to
     electronic communications without the consent of the parties involved
     or an order from an authorized official.
   Section 20: CCTV cameras at public places may not be installed in
     toilets, bathrooms, or changing rooms, and notice of installation must
     be given.
   Section 21: No electronic means, photography, or method may be used for
     surveillance or espionage of a person's residence or office.
   Section 22: No drone may be used to obtain secret information of a
     public body, protected site, or person's residence without permission.

3. CHAPTER 10 — COLLECTION AND PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION
     (Sections 23-28)
   Section 23: No one except an official authorized under law may collect,
     store, protect, analyze, process, or publish a person's personal
     information. Where information is collected with consent (e.g. for
     research), the collector must disclose the purpose, content, and
     nature of the information being collected.
   Section 25: A public body holding personal information must protect it
     and make appropriate arrangements against unauthorized access, use,
     change, disclosure, or transmission.
   Section 26: Personal information collected by a public body or body
     corporate shall not be used or disclosed without the consent of the
     concerned person, except in specified circumstances (e.g. the purpose
     for which it was collected, or a written demand in the course of an
     investigation).
   Section 27: A public body shall not process sensitive information except
     as provided by law.
   Section 28: A person may apply to correct information they believe is
     inaccurate.

4. CHAPTER 11 — OFFENCE AND PUNISHMENT (Sections 29-32)
   Section 29(1): Lists the specific acts that constitute an offence under
     this Act (violations of the body/residence/property/document/data/
     correspondence/character/electronic-means/collection provisions listed
     above).
   Section 29(2): Punishment of imprisonment not exceeding three years, or a
     fine not exceeding thirty thousand (30,000) rupees, or both, for the
     offences referred to in sub-section (1).
   Section 31: Compensation may be awarded if any damage, loss, or injury is
     caused to a person by an offence under this Act.
   Section 32: A public official who fails in their duty under this Act may
     face departmental punishment.

[VERIFY] This is the source of the "3 years imprisonment or NPR 30,000 fine"
penalty figure — CLAUDE.md previously attributed this penalty to the
Electronic Transactions Act 2063, which is incorrect; the ETA's own
confidentiality-breach penalty (Section 48) is a fine not exceeding NPR
10,000 or imprisonment not exceeding 2 years. See electronic_transactions_act
_2063_key_clauses.txt and ERRORS_TO_FIX.md for this correction.
