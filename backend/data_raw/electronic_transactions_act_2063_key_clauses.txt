This document quotes the actual Act text (numbered sections, Chapter 9 -
"Offence Relating To Computer"), not a paraphrase. Penalty amounts below are
the Act's own figures — do not confuse with the Privacy Act 2075's penalty
(see privacy_act_2075_summary.txt), which is a different, separate law with
its own three-year/NPR-30,000 penalty for different offences.

1. PREAMBLE AND SCOPE (Section 1)
   This Act extends throughout Nepal and also applies to any person residing
   anywhere who commits an offence in contravention of this Act, where the
   computer, computer system, or computer network involved is located in
   Nepal (Section 55).

2. CHAPTER 9 — OFFENCE RELATING TO COMPUTER (Sections 44-59)
   Section 44 — To Pirate, Destroy or Alter Computer Source Code: knowingly
     or with mala fide intention pirating, destroying, or altering computer
     source code — imprisonment not exceeding 3 years or fine not exceeding
     NPR 200,000, or both.
   Section 45 — Unauthorized Access in Computer Materials: accessing a
     computer's programme, information, or data without authorization, or
     contrary to the scope of authorization granted — fine not exceeding
     NPR 200,000 or imprisonment not exceeding 3 years, or both, depending
     on the seriousness of the offence.
   Section 46 — Damage to any Computer and Information System: knowingly
     and with mala fide intention causing wrongful loss or damage by
     destroying, damaging, deleting, or altering computer information —
     fine not exceeding NPR 2,000 and imprisonment not exceeding 3 years, or
     both.
   Section 47 — Publication of Illegal Materials in Electronic Form:
     publishing/displaying prohibited material via computer or internet —
     fine not exceeding NPR 100,000 or imprisonment not exceeding 5 years,
     or both; repeat offences add 1.5x the previous punishment each time.
   Section 48 — Confidentiality to Divulge: a person with authorized access
     to records/data who discloses their confidentiality to an unauthorized
     person — fine not exceeding NPR 10,000 or imprisonment not exceeding
     2 years, or both.
   Section 52 — To Commit Computer Fraud: creating/publishing a fraudulent
     digital signature certificate or acquiring financial benefit through
     fraud involving accounts/cards — fine not exceeding NPR 100,000 or
     imprisonment not exceeding 2 years, or both, plus recovery of the
     financial benefit acquired.
   Section 53 — Abetment to Commit Computer Related Offence: fine not
     exceeding NPR 50,000 or imprisonment not exceeding 6 months, or both.
   Section 56 — Confiscation: any computer, system, or device used to
     commit a computer offence under this Act is liable to confiscation.
   Section 58 — Other Punishment: any violation with no separately
     specified penalty — fine not exceeding NPR 50,000 or imprisonment not
     exceeding 6 months, or both.

3. CHAPTER 8 — NETWORK SERVICE PROVIDER LIABILITY (Sections 42-43)
   Section 43 — a network/internet service provider is not liable for
   third-party content made available through their service, unless they
   had knowledge that the content contravened this Act.

4. CHAPTER 10 — INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY TRIBUNAL (Section 60 onward)
   Government of Nepal constitutes an Information Technology Tribunal and
   an Appellate Tribunal to adjudicate offences under Chapter 9, rather than
   ordinary courts.
