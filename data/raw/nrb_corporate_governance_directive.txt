VERBATIM EXCERPT — NRB Directive No. 2 on Corporate Governance
Source: Nepal Rastra Bank, Unified Directives 2082 (2026), Directive No. 2
Official: https://www.nrb.org.np
================================================================================

This document summarises the key provisions of NRB Directive No. 2, which
establishes the corporate governance framework for all Class A, B, C, and D
banks and financial institutions (BFIs) licensed by Nepal Rastra Bank.
NRB issued this directive under Section 79 of the Nepal Rastra Bank Act, 2058.

1. BOARD OF DIRECTORS — COMPOSITION AND RESPONSIBILITIES

   Section 1: Board Composition
   - Every BFI must have a Board of Directors comprising at least 5 and
     no more than 11 members.
   - At least one-third of board members must be independent directors.
   - The board must include a balance of executive, non-executive, and
     independent directors to ensure effective oversight.
   - Directors must meet fit-and-proper criteria prescribed by NRB,
     including educational qualifications, professional experience, and
     clean criminal and credit history.

   Section 2: Board Responsibilities
   - The board is responsible for approving the BFI's strategic direction,
     business plans, and annual budgets.
   - The board must ensure adequate internal controls and risk management
     systems are in place.
   - The board must approve and review the BFI's policies including credit
     risk, market risk, liquidity risk, operational risk, and compliance
     policies.
   - The board must ensure compliance with all applicable laws, regulations,
     and NRB directives.

   Section 3: Board Committees
   - Every BFI must establish the following mandatory committees:
     (a) Audit Committee — comprising at least 3 members, all non-executive,
         with at least one independent director as chairperson. Responsible
         for overseeing financial reporting, internal audit, and external
         audit functions.
     (b) Risk Management Committee — responsible for overseeing the BFI's
         risk management framework, including credit, market, liquidity,
         operational, and IT/cybersecurity risks.
     (c) Remuneration Committee — responsible for setting compensation
         policies for senior management and board members.
     (d) Nomination Committee — responsible for recommending candidates
         for board positions based on fit-and-proper criteria.
   - Committees must meet at least quarterly and report to the full board.

2. FIT-AND-PROPER CRITERIA

   Section 4: Director Qualifications
   - No person shall be appointed as a director if they:
     (a) Have been convicted of a criminal offence involving moral turpitude.
     (b) Have been declared bankrupt or have outstanding unpaid debts.
     (c) Have been blacklisted by NRB or any other financial regulator.
     (d) Have been removed from a position in any financial institution
         for misconduct.
   - Directors must disclose any conflicts of interest to the board and
     must recuse themselves from decisions where they have a personal interest.
   - No person may serve as a director in more than 3 BFIs simultaneously.

3. DISCLOSURE AND TRANSPARENCY

   Section 5: Mandatory Disclosures
   - BFIs must collect and submit detailed disclosures when appointing
     directors, CEOs, or senior management, including:
     (a) Self-declaration of legal compliance under applicable laws.
     (b) Full family details and background information.
     (c) Criminal background disclosure, both domestic and international.
     (d) Details of ongoing lawsuits, investigations, or dispute proceedings.
     (e) Information on outstanding government dues or court-ordered liabilities.
     (f) Blacklisting status in Nepal or abroad related to credit or loans.
     (g) Conflict of interest declarations.
     (h) Disclosure of foreign assets or beneficial ownership (for Nepali citizens).
     (i) Detailed professional history, including employment record,
         responsibilities, awards, and penalties.
     (j) Financial soundness declarations.
   - Existing executives must also submit the same disclosures.
   - Any changes in disclosed information must be updated and reported to NRB.

   Section 6: Financial Reporting
   - All BFIs must publish a summarized interim financial report within
     15 days of the end of each quarter in a national daily newspaper.
   - Full financial details must be uploaded to the institution's website.
   - Annual audited financial statements must be presented at the annual
     general meeting (AGM) within 6 months of the fiscal year end.

4. SHAREHOLDER PROTECTIONS

   Section 7: Promoter Shareholding
   - When shareholding exceeds 5% of paid-up capital or NPR 2.5 million,
     BFIs must collect detailed beneficial ownership information and submit
     it to NRB.
   - Required disclosures include identity details, family background,
     criminal history, legal disputes, tax liabilities, blacklist status,
     conflict of interest declarations, foreign asset ownership,
     professional background, and financial capacity.
   - For institutional investors holding stakes in banks, the rule extends
     to individuals who directly or indirectly control 15% or more of
     the investing entity.

   Section 8: Related-Party Transactions
   - Loans and advances to related parties must be approved by the board
     and disclosed in the financial statements.
   - Related-party loans must not exceed prescribed limits as a percentage
     of the BFI's capital.

5. RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK

   Section 9: Enterprise Risk Management
   - Every BFI must establish a comprehensive risk management framework
     covering:
     (a) Credit risk — policies for loan approval, classification,
         provisioning, and concentration limits.
     (b) Market risk — policies for interest rate risk, foreign exchange
         risk, and securities portfolio risk.
     (c) Liquidity risk — policies for liquidity buffer, stress testing,
         and contingency funding plans.
     (d) Operational risk — policies for internal controls, business
         continuity planning, and incident management.
     (e) IT and cybersecurity risk — policies for information security,
         data protection, and cyber resilience.
   - The board must appoint a Chief Risk Officer (CRO) who reports
     directly to the Risk Management Committee.

6. INTERNAL AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE

   Section 10: Internal Audit
   - Every BFI must establish an independent internal audit function that
     reports directly to the Audit Committee.
   - Internal audit must cover all operations and must conduct risk-based
     annual audit plans.
   - The head of internal audit must be appointed by the board and cannot
     be removed without NRB's approval.

   Section 11: Compliance Function
   - Every BFI must appoint a Compliance Officer responsible for ensuring
     compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and NRB directives.
   - The Compliance Officer must submit quarterly compliance reports to
     the Audit Committee and NRB.

7. PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

   Section 12: Enforcement Actions
   - NRB may impose penalties including:
     (a) Monetary fines up to NPR 10 million per violation.
     (b) Removal of directors or officers found guilty of misconduct.
     (c) Restriction on dividend payments and branch expansion.
     (d) Suspension or revocation of the BFI's license in severe cases.
   - NRB may also initiate criminal proceedings against individuals
     involved in fraud, embezzlement, or willful negligence.

8. GOVERNANCE CHALLENGES IN NEPAL

   Section 13: Known Issues
   - Board politicization: Board positions are sometimes awarded based on
     political connections rather than professional competence.
   - Weak enforcement: While NRB has comprehensive directives, enforcement
     has been inconsistent, particularly for smaller BFIs.
   - Limited independent director pool: Nepal's financial sector has a
     relatively small pool of qualified independent directors.
   - Related-party lending: Historical concerns about excessive lending
     to related parties of board members and major shareholders.
   - Transparency gaps: Some BFIs have been slow to adopt international
     disclosure and reporting standards.

   Section 14: Recent Reforms (2025-2026)
   - NRB has tightened disclosure requirements for bank executives and
     major shareholders (Unified Directives 2082, June 2026).
   - Continuous reporting requirements now mandate real-time updates
     whenever disclosed information changes.
   - Stricter beneficial ownership rules for institutional investors
     with 15%+ stakes in BFIs.
   - Enhanced oversight of IT governance and cybersecurity risk as part
     of the broader enterprise risk management framework.
