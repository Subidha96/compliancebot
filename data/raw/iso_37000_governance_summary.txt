VERBATIM SUMMARY — ISO 37000:2021 Governance of Organizations
Source: International Organization for Standardization (ISO)
Official: https://www.iso.org/standard/65036.html
================================================================================

ISO 37000:2021 provides guidance on the governance of organizations. It
provides principles and key aspects of practices to guide governing bodies
and governing groups on how to meet their responsibilities so that the
organizations they govern can fulfil their purpose. Published in 2021, it
is the first international standard for organizational governance.

APPLICABILITY:
   The standard is applicable to all organizations regardless of type, size,
   location, structure or purpose. It is also intended for stakeholders
   involved in, or impacted by, the organization and its governance.

1. THE GOVERNANCE OF ORGANIZATIONS

   4.1 General
   Governance is defined as the system by which an organization is directed
   and held to account. It encompasses the systems, structures, processes,
   and practices that determine how the organization makes decisions, sets
   objectives, and monitors performance.

   4.2 Integrated Governance
   Good governance integrates purpose, strategy, and oversight across all
   levels of the organization. It is not a separate function but is embedded
   in how the organization operates.

   4.3 The Governing Body
   The governing body (board of directors, council, or equivalent) is
   responsible for the governance of the organization. Its role includes:
   - Setting the organization's purpose, vision, and strategic direction.
   - Establishing governance policies and ethical standards.
   - Overseeing risk management and internal controls.
   - Ensuring accountability and transparency.
   - Engaging with stakeholders.

2. PRINCIPLES OF GOVERNANCE

   ISO 37000 distills governance into 11 core principles organized into
   three tiers: 1 Primary Principle, 4 Foundational Principles, and
   6 Enabling Principles.

   6.1 PURPOSE (Primary Principle)
   The pursuit of purpose is at the centre of all organizations and is of
   primary importance for governance. Good governance lays the foundation
   for the fulfilment of the purpose of the organization in an ethical,
   effective and responsible manner in line with stakeholder expectations.

   6.2 VALUE GENERATION (Foundational Principle)
   The governing body should clarify the value generation objectives and
   govern so that these objectives are met. This requires defining a clear
   and transparent value generation model that defines, creates, delivers
   and sustains appropriate value for all stakeholders.

   6.3 STRATEGY (Foundational Principle)
   The governing body should direct and engage with the organizational strategy
   to fulfil the organizational purpose. The board sets strategic outcomes,
   establishes governance policies to guide strategy development, engages in
   strategic planning and dynamically steers the strategy through personal,
   direct involvement.

   6.4 OVERSIGHT (Foundational Principle)
   The governing body's role and responsibility includes overseeing and
   ensuring the right organizational controls. For the first time, clarity
   is given at a global level on the internal control system and the
   assurance processes. Oversight includes ensuring that an internal control
   system is implemented and assuring itself that the governance system is
   appropriately designed and operating as intended.

   6.5 ACCOUNTABILITY (Foundational Principle)
   The governing body is not "above the law" and neither are those to whom
   the governing body has delegated. Accountability at all levels is a key
   aspect of governance. It is demonstrated through reports, disclosures,
   effective stakeholder engagement, and applying improvements.

   6.6 STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT (Enabling Principle)
   The governing body should understand its stakeholders, establish clear
   criteria to determine the relevance of stakeholder expectations, ensure
   effective relationships are established and maintained, and that
   expectations become an effective part of organizational decision-making.

   6.7 LEADERSHIP (Enabling Principle)
   The governing body should lead the organization ethically and effectively
   and ensure such leadership throughout the organization. The board should
   set the tone for an ethical organizational culture, lead by example to
   create a positive culture, and engender trust and cooperation among
   stakeholders.

   6.8 DATA AND DECISIONS (Enabling Principle)
   The governing body should recognize data as a strategic asset. It should
   ensure that decisions are based on credible, relevant, and timely
   information. The board should establish processes for collecting,
   analyzing, and using data to inform governance decisions.

   6.9 RISK GOVERNANCE (Enabling Principle)
   The governing body should oversee the identification, assessment, and
   management of risks that may affect the organization's ability to fulfil
   its purpose. This includes strategic, operational, financial, compliance,
   and reputational risks.

   6.10 SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY (Enabling Principle)
   The governing body should ensure that the organization operates in a
   socially responsible manner, considering the impact of its decisions
   on society and the environment.

   6.11 VIABILITY AND PERFORMANCE OVER TIME (Enabling Principle)
   The governing body should ensure the organization's long-term viability
   and sustainable performance by balancing short-term results with
   long-term value creation.

3. GOVERNANCE OUTCOMES

   Effective application of these principles leads to the following outcomes:
   - Ethical and effective organizational behaviour.
   - Sustainable value creation for stakeholders.
   - Trust and legitimacy with stakeholders.
   - Resilience in the face of change and adversity.
   - Accountability and transparency in decision-making.

4. APPLICATION STEPS

   Applying governance in conformity with ISO 37000 involves:
   1. Leadership and Commitment — obtain leadership commitment for governance.
   2. Define Governance Objectives — align with strategic goals and stakeholder
      interests.
   3. Establish Governance Structures — define roles, responsibilities, and
      decision-making mechanisms.
   4. Risk Management and Compliance — implement processes to identify, assess,
      and mitigate governance risks.
   5. Ethical Standards and Integrity — establish codes of conduct for all
      members of the organization.
   6. Stakeholder Engagement — identify and engage with key stakeholders.
   7. Transparency and Accountability — provide stakeholders with access to
      relevant information.
   8. Performance Monitoring — establish KPIs to measure governance effectiveness.
   9. Continual Improvement — review and update governance structures regularly.
   10. Training and Development — enhance governance knowledge and skills.

RELEVANCE TO GRC PRACTITIONERS:
   - ISO 37000 provides a framework for organizations to assess and improve
     their governance practices.
   - It complements ISO 27001 (information security governance) by providing
     the broader organizational governance context.
   - For Nepal, ISO 37000 offers an international benchmark for evaluating
     the governance maturity of companies, banks, and other organizations.
