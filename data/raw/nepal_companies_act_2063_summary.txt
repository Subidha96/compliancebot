VERBATIM EXCERPT — Companies Act, 2063 (2006), Key Corporate Governance Provisions
Source: Nepal Law Commission
Official: https://lawcommission.gov.np/content/12157/12157-company-act-2063/
================================================================================

The Companies Act 2063 (2006) is the primary corporate law governing company
formation, management, and compliance in Nepal. It replaced the Companies Act
2053 and consists of 21 chapters and 188 sections. This summary covers the
key corporate governance provisions relevant to GRC compliance.

1. TYPES OF COMPANIES

   Section 3: Company Classification
   - Private Company: Maximum 50 shareholders; shares not publicly traded.
     Examples: startups, family-owned businesses.
   - Public Company: Minimum 7 promoters; shares publicly traded on NEPSE.
     Examples: banks, insurance companies, listed companies.
   - Single Shareholder Company: Introduced by the 2063 Act; owned by one
     person; no mandatory board meetings required.

2. BOARD OF DIRECTORS

   Section 56: Board Composition
   - Public companies must have a board of 3 to 11 directors.
   - At least one director must be an independent director.
   - Directors are elected by shareholders at the annual general meeting
     or appointed by promoters in the case of a new company.

   Section 57: Director Qualifications
   - A person shall not be appointed as a director if they:
     (a) Are of unsound mind or have been declared of unsound mind by a court.
     (b) Are an undischarged insolvent.
     (c) Have been convicted of a criminal offence involving moral turpitude.
     (d) Have been disqualified under any prevailing law.

   Section 58: Duties of Directors
   - Directors must act in good faith and in the best interest of the company.
   - Directors must exercise due care, skill, and diligence.
   - Directors must disclose any conflict of interest to the board.
   - Directors must not make secret profits from their position.
   - Directors must comply with the Companies Act, memorandum of association,
     and articles of association.

   Section 59: Independent Directors
   - Public companies must appoint at least one independent director.
   - An independent director must not have any material relationship with
     the company (e.g., not be an employee, major shareholder, or business
     partner of the company).
   - Independent directors serve a term of up to 4 years and may be
     reappointed.

3. BOARD COMMITTEES

   Section 60: Audit Committee
   - Public companies with paid-up capital of NPR 30 million or more must
     establish an audit committee.
   - The audit committee must comprise at least 3 members, all non-executive
     directors, with at least one independent director as chairperson.
   - The audit committee is responsible for:
     (a) Overseeing the financial reporting process.
     (b) Reviewing internal controls and risk management.
     (c) Recommending the appointment and removal of external auditors.
     (d) Reviewing the findings of internal and external audits.

4. SHAREHOLDER PROTECTIONS

   Section 61: Annual General Meeting (AGM)
   - Every company must hold an AGM within 6 months of the end of its
     fiscal year.
   - Notice of the AGM must be given to all shareholders at least 21
     days in advance.
   - Shareholders have the right to receive the annual financial statements,
     auditor's report, and any other information required by the Act.

   Section 62: Voting Rights
   - Every shareholder has the right to vote at general meetings.
   - Voting may be by show of hands or by poll (if demanded by shareholders).
   - A quorum of at least 2 shareholders holding at least 10% of paid-up
     capital is required for a valid general meeting.

   Section 63: Related-Party Transactions
   - Transactions between the company and its directors or related parties
     must be disclosed to the board and approved by shareholders.
   - The board must ensure that related-party transactions are conducted
     at arm's length and in the best interest of the company.

   Section 64: Shareholder Remedies
   - A shareholder may apply to the court if the company's affairs are
     being conducted in a manner oppressive to minority shareholders.
   - The court may order the purchase of shares, winding up of the company,
     or other relief as it deems fit.

5. FINANCIAL REPORTING AND AUDIT

   Section 115: Accounts and Audit
   - Every company must maintain proper books of accounts using the
     double-entry system.
   - Financial statements must be prepared in accordance with Nepal
     Financial Reporting Standards (NFRS) or International Financial
     Reporting Standards (IFRS).
   - Annual financial statements must be audited by a practicing chartered
     accountant registered with the Institute of Chartered Accountants
     of Nepal (ICAN).

   Section 116: Auditor's Report
   - The auditor must submit a report to the shareholders on the financial
     statements, stating whether they present a true and fair view of the
     company's financial position.
   - The auditor must report any material irregularities, fraud, or
     non-compliance with the Companies Act to the company board and
     the Office of the Company Registrar.

6. INVESTOR PROTECTION FUND

   Section 117: Unclaimed Dividends
   - Any dividend that remains unclaimed for 3 consecutive years must be
     transferred to an Investor Protection Fund established under this Act.
   - The fund is used to protect the interests of investors and to
     compensate shareholders in cases of fraud or mismanagement.

7. PENALTIES AND ENFORCEMENT

   Section 180: Offences
   - Penalties for non-compliance with the Companies Act include:
     (a) Fines ranging from NPR 10,000 to NPR 500,000 depending on the
         nature and severity of the offence.
     (b) Imprisonment for up to 3 years for serious offences such as
         fraud, embezzlement, or willful misrepresentation.
     (c) Disqualification of directors found guilty of misconduct.

8. 2025-2026 AMENDMENTS

   Key Changes:
   - Companies may now issue shares for non-cash contributions (IP, services,
     know-how, goodwill) based on independent valuation and special
     shareholder resolution.
   - Share issuance caps raised: 20% general, 40% for startups.
   - Cross-appointments of directors between related companies now permitted.
   - One-time 90% waiver on late-filing penalties to encourage compliance.
   - Faster digital registration streamlined to 15 days.
